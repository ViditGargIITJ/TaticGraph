AI Political Bias Explainer

Feature 1: Analyze Article for Political Bias

Example Article:

The government’s recent economic policies have propelled India’s global standing, experts say. Massive infrastructure projects and investor-friendly reforms have placed the country on a high growth trajectory. Meanwhile, opposition parties criticize the lack of inclusivity in these initiatives, calling them elitist. However, the public sentiment, gauged through recent polls, appears overwhelmingly in support of the ruling party’s agenda.

Bias Analysis:
Bias Detected: Right-Leaning
Indicators:
  • Favorable Framing: Language that portrays government initiatives as unquestionably positive, e.g., "investor-friendly" and "high growth trajectory".
  • Marginalization: Opposing viewpoints are presented but undermined or labeled, e.g., describing critics as calling policies "elitist".
  • Appeal to Majority: Using popularity through polls to validate one side, possibly marginalizing dissent.
Recommendations:
  • Cross-check facts with alternate sources.
  • Read opposing viewpoints for a balanced understanding.
  • Be mindful of language that may sway perception subtly.
